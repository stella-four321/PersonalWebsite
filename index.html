Me as an Engineer

Hello and welcome to my personal website! Just a small introduction of myself, I am currently a 3rd year majoring in electrical engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology. I fell in love with engineering when I realized that it is a very creative and interdisciplinary subject field. For example, I am currently in a research making artistic robotic painting system. Since I am an artistic and creative person, I love how I can combine the more artistic subject to a more concrete field, like engineering and robotics.

Being an engineer at Georgia Tech, I get the opportunity to explore. I once worked with a graduate student in the material science department building lithium-ion batteries. It was intriguing to see a coin-cell battery that I build, that I spend days synthesizing the materials, having a voltage drop across the battery! I am curious and I am willing to learn any new skills provided to me. For example, I self-learned HTML and Java to build this personal website. Furthermore, I am a Peer Instructor at an interdisciplinary makerspace on campus in which I help students fascilitate their personal projects. To become a Peer Instructor, I get to learn how to operate all the equipment in the makerspace, including PCB manufacturing, 3D printing, laser cutting, wood working, embedded systems, and electronics benchtop.

Passion for Space

I've always had this dream to become an astronaut. Maybe it seems to be a far-fatched one, but Astronaut Hadfield inspires me to reach for the impossible. He inspires me, as an electrical engineer, to become a critical-thinker and a problem-solver while I am building and testing personal innovative projects and doing labs in school. I challenge myself everyday to create and learn new technical skills to become a skilled engineer with the mentality that perhaps one day, I will be competent to take on the role of an astronaut.

Building rockets into space is one of my passions and it is what I intend to do with my life. I love to explore and go on adventures. The vast universe fascinates me and keeps me questioning what’s out there. Always enthusiastic about searching for answers, I long to explore the universe. By creating and sending flight vehicles for space exploration, I hope to find answers to my questions and bring back new knowledge down on Earth to help mankind advance and improve.




I've always been a very musical person. I started playing the piano since I was eight years old and the violin a fews years after. In high school, I played a lot of classical pieces on the piano and I was also playing the violin in our school's orchestra. At home, you can find me singing in my room, writing my own indie pop music on my guitar, ukulele, and the piano. I started recording my own music and posting them on my YouTube channel to share to my friends and family.

My favorite performance I did was during my high school's concert where I played Paganini Etude No.6 by Liszt. Everytime I played this piece, I would get an intense rush through my body because of its highly technical and complex composition. Though I don't spend as much time playing classical piano after going to college, I never stopped writing songs and creating music. It is not only my hobby but also a source for me to express my emotions using words and melodies.

Future Careers

I will be interning at NASA as an electrical engineer working on drones in the Magnetic Seperation project in the Spring. The project involves the use of patterned magnets to do all sorts of two-body attachment and separation techniques. The project spans aeronautics and space. As for me personally, I will be working on the electrical portions to our drone recovery gantry, the heavy-lift unit (500 lb rated release), zero-g aircraft demo units... Furthermore, I will intern at Qualcomm as an Embedded Software Engineer for the summer. These two oppurtunities will help me figure out what I really want to do in my future careers, either more hardware hands-on testing or the more coding software part of electrical engineering. After the internships, I plan to finish my Bachelor degree in Electrical Engineering then do a Master's degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology.
